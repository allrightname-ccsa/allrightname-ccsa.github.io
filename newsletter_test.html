At the Helm
By Charles Bell -Commodore

Spring 2014

After a brutal winter and a well-attended Winter Social, Opening Day is April 27th

This year, Starting Practice is the first event. This is a great time to renew acquaintances, compare craft and shake-out our boats in starting line strategy. And if you have a boat in the CCSA Boatyard, please remember your obligation to join the rest of your colleagues as we clean-up after the ravages of winter on Saturday April 5th. Donuts and coffee will be provided. Bring your tire pump.

Three things are different this year:

1 . We are improving last year's introduction of the High Point Scoring system. We will be scoring the best of twenty races, not every single race. This will encourage the flexibility of sharing crews, changing boats and trying new things without being "hell-bent" to load up points in an open- ended scoring system.

2. We have added a "Come Sail with Us Fun Race"; CCSA's first invitational. Bring your boat..... and a friend. Invite others, with or without boats....any excuse to include guests to sail with us at Creve Coeur Lake.

3. We are making a concerted effort to demonstrate to St. Louis County Parks Department that CCSA continues to value and use our sailing facilities. And as the second most active sailing association in Missouri, we are anticipating all the promised scheduled improvements of the ramps, seawall, docks, boat parking and restrooms.

See you in the Boatyard.
- CB

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

2014 Race Scoring System
By Matt Moliter		  

Did you ever wonder how we score our races? For each race you are timed from the start to finish. Your time is then adjusted based upon your US Sailing Basic Portsmouth Number and this adjusted time is then used to determine your place in the race.
In 2013, we began using the CHIPS High Point System: For each race you get points (0 to 1 00) based on which position you
placed and how many boats are in the race. The better your position, the more points you get. The more boats that race, the
more points you get. For your total year’s score, add up your scores from each race. In 2013 there was no cap on the number of races. In 2014 there will be a cap of your 20 best races. The boat with the most points wins.

- MM
